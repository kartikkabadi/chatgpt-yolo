# Motion Storyboard B — "The Control Rail"

Alternative motion storyboard for the YOLO for ChatGPT launch video (Wave B, Storyboard B).
This is an independent creative direction. It follows the required 7-scene structure, timing,
and constraints from the orchestration brief (§13) while proposing a distinct visual and
motion concept from Storyboard A.

---

## Summary

**Concept:** One continuous workspace instead of seven separate scenes. The YOLO queue lives
as a **persistent vertical rail on the left third** of the frame, and the ChatGPT conversation
occupies the right two-thirds, for the entire runtime. Rather than hard-cutting between
disconnected product shots, the piece **pans, masks, and reframes within a single coherent
workspace** so the queue is always at least partially visible. The queue-rail is the spatial
anchor and the literal embodiment of the core promise — you can always see what happens next
and you stay in control.

**Why this direction:**
- The persistent rail makes "stay in control" a *structural* property of the video, not just a
  caption. The viewer never loses sight of the mechanism.
- Continuous in-workspace motion (pan / mask / reframe) reads as calm and trustworthy, which
  matches a reliability-first, local-first product better than punchy full-screen cuts.
- Kinetic type is confined to a **lower-third caption band** that never covers product UI,
  directly satisfying the "transitions must not obscure UI" and "no tiny text" constraints.

**Palette / tone:** Dark editorial. Near-black background (`#0E1014`), panel greys, one
controlled accent blue (`#60A5FA`) for YOLO chrome and progress, green (`#4AC99B`) for
done/safe states, amber (`#F0BD5A`) for paused/ambiguous states. Restrained, product-honest.

**Runtime:** 42s primary (comfortably inside the 35–45s window), same timing map reused for the
1:1 square cut.

**Format targets (from brief):** 1920×1080 16:9 @ 30fps H.264 `yuv420p`, fast-start, < 35 MB
primary; 1080×1080 real square recomposition as secondary (the rail narrows and the caption band
moves up — not a blind crop).

**Fully understandable muted.** Audio is a supportive layer only (see Audio notes).

---

## Distinctiveness vs. Storyboard A

This storyboard is written to be a genuinely different option, not a re-skin:

- **Spatial model:** a single persistent left queue-rail + continuous in-workspace camera,
  versus per-scene full-frame product shots with hard cuts.
- **Transitions:** masked reframes and horizontal pans *within* one canvas, versus scene-to-scene
  cross-dissolves/cuts.
- **Typography:** kinetic text lives exclusively in a lower-third caption band that never
  overlaps UI, versus center-stage kinetic type.
- **Palette:** committed dark editorial with a single accent, versus a lighter/product-neutral
  treatment.

The orchestrator can select whichever direction tests stronger; both honor the same script beats.

## Scene-by-scene breakdown

Timing uses the brief's beat map. Frame sketches for each scene are in
[`frames/`](frames/) (`scene-1.png` … `scene-7.png`), generated by
[`frames/make_frames.py`](frames/make_frames.py). They are rough layout guides, not final renders.

### Scene 1 — Problem · 0:00–0:04

- **Visual:** Open already inside the workspace. The right panel shows a long ChatGPT assistant
  response that has just finished. The left rail is present but **dimmed/inactive** — a greyed
  placeholder reading "You keep coming back / just to say 'continue'." The composer sits empty
  with a ghost "continue" the user is about to type again, manually. The problem is framed as
  repetitive manual babysitting.
- **On-screen text (exact):**
  > Stop babysitting long ChatGPT tasks.
- **Key motion:** Cold open — the first meaningful message is on screen by ~0:00.3. No logo
  animation. A single slow settle: the finished response block "lands" (2px drop + fade-in over
  ~10 frames), the ghost cursor blinks once in the empty composer. The dimmed rail sits quietly
  to establish the space the queue will fill.
- **Audio notes:** Silence or a single soft low tone under the caption's arrival. No stinger.

### Scene 2 — Queue · 0:04–0:12

- **Visual:** The left rail **activates** (dim → full contrast) and three next instructions
  insert into it, top to bottom. Header shows "YOLO · Queue" and "3 queued · in order". Each item
  shows an index, label, and `QUEUED` state. A brief drag re-order gesture swaps items 2 and 3 to
  demonstrate ordering, then the top item flips to a `SENDING` state and its border lights accent
  blue — a controlled transition toward sending.
- **On-screen text (exact):**
  > Queue what should happen next.
- **Key motion:** Queue-item insertion is the hero motion — items slide in from the rail edge
  with restrained ease-out (~8 frames each, staggered). Drag-reorder shows direct manipulation
  (item lifts 3px, shadow, settles). The `SENDING` state is a border-color transition + a small
  count tick (3 → sending), never a flashy effect.
- **Audio notes:** Three soft, discrete UI ticks on each item insertion (distinct pitches,
  ascending), one lower confirming tick on the send transition. Conservative mix.

### Scene 3 — Composer actions · 0:12–0:19

- **Visual:** Camera reframes right, and the **YOLO action palette** opens upward from the
  ChatGPT composer (triggered by `/` in an empty composer). Palette lists real actions with short
  descriptions: `/goal`, `/loop`, `/review`, `/continue`. The rail stays partly visible at the
  left edge so continuity is preserved. A concrete bounded command is composed: `/loop 4 improve
  the failing module` (a real, bounded example).
- **On-screen text (exact):**
  > Start from the composer.
- **Key motion:** Palette rises with a masked reveal (clip-in from the composer top, ~10 frames).
  A keyboard/`/` cue appears briefly. Selection highlight glides down the list to `/loop`, then
  the command types into the composer with a restrained per-character cadence (not frantic).
- **Audio notes:** Soft "open" whoosh (short, low) for the palette; muted key clicks under the
  typed command. Optional and subtle.

### Scene 4 — Bounded workflow · 0:19–0:28

- **Visual:** The `/loop 4` workflow is running. The right panel header shows a **segmented
  progress bar of exactly four segments** and the label "Turn 2 of 4". Two segments are filled
  (green), two remain. Below sit visible **Pause / Edit / Stop** controls. The rail now shows the
  workflow's queued turns advancing. Crucially, the workflow **visibly reaches its bound**: the
  bar fills to 4 of 4 and resolves to a `DONE` state (green), OR pauses on demand — never loops
  endlessly.
- **On-screen text (exact):**
  > Bounded workflows. Visible state. Your controls.
- **Key motion:** Progress segments fill one at a time (Turn 1→2→3→4), each a short accent-blue
  → green fill (~6 frames). "Turn N of 4" counter increments in place. The Pause/Edit/Stop trio
  gets a brief hover/focus ring pass to show they are live. Final segment fills and the bar
  settles into a completed/paused resting state — the motion literally stops to prove boundedness.
- **Audio notes:** A soft segmented "tick" per turn (four ticks total), then one gentle resolving
  chord/tone when the bound is reached. No triumphant trailer swell.

### Scene 5 — Optional GitHub setup · 0:28–0:35

- **Visual:** The right panel shows an **optional connected GitHub app / repository context** in
  the ChatGPT conversation, marked by a clear chip. The left rail shows YOLO's queued **coding
  sequence**: inspect → implement → validate → review → summarize (five items). The boundary is
  explicit: GitHub is a thing *inside ChatGPT*; YOLO is the rail moving the prompts.
- **On-screen text (exact):**
  > Connect GitHub to ChatGPT.

  then:
  > YOLO keeps the next steps moving.

  Persistent small label (legible, on screen long enough to read):
  > Optional GitHub app · Connected directly to ChatGPT
- **Key motion:** The GitHub context chip slides in on the ChatGPT side with a masked reveal.
  The five coding-sequence items cascade into the rail (staggered ~6 frames each). A subtle
  connecting cue clarifies direction: the chip lives on the **ChatGPT** panel, the sequence lives
  on the **YOLO** rail — they are visually separated, never merged.
- **Audio notes:** One soft "connect" confirmation tone as the chip appears; light ticks as the
  sequence items land. **No** sound or motion implying YOLO itself authenticates to GitHub.
- **Boundary guardrail:** Do not draw YOLO holding GitHub credentials, tokens, or repo access.
  The label and separation must survive any re-edit. YOLO never receives GitHub credentials or
  repository data.

### Scene 6 — Reliability · 0:35–0:40

- **Visual:** Rapid but readable product truths, each tied to an actual product state rather than
  an abstract icon. Three chips appear in sequence over the right panel while the rail stays
  visible:
  1. Draft protected — shown as the composer keeping existing user text untouched.
  2. Ambiguous? It pauses — shown as a workflow entering a paused/held (amber) state, not retrying.
  3. Local by default — shown as a `chrome.storage.local` / no-telemetry state.
- **On-screen text (exact):**
  > Draft protected.

  > Ambiguous? It pauses.

  > Local by default.
- **Key motion:** Each truth is a quick chip-in (~7 frames) with a matching product-state
  micro-demo behind it, held ~1.2s each so it is comfortably readable. Restrained; no rapid-fire
  strobing. The amber pause state deliberately *stops* motion to reinforce fail-closed behavior.
- **Audio notes:** One soft confirming tick per chip (three total). The pause chip uses a slightly
  lower, softer tone to read as "held," not "error."

### Scene 7 — End card · 0:40–0:45

- **Visual:** The workspace resolves: the rail and panel gracefully collapse/mask toward center
  and the composition becomes a clean, centered end card on the dark background. Everything is
  static and held long enough to read and act on.
- **On-screen text (exact):**
  > YOLO for ChatGPT

  > Queue the next steps. Stay in control.

  Supporting line:
  > Local-first · Open source · No telemetry

  CTA:
  > github.com/kartikkabadi/chatgpt-yolo

  Secondary CTA:
  > Sponsor development

  Legal line:
  > Independent project. Not affiliated with OpenAI.
- **Key motion:** A single unifying collapse (rail + panel mask inward over ~12 frames) into the
  title, then the supporting/CTA lines fade up in a short stagger. Then everything **holds
  static** for ~3s. No looping motion under the CTA so the URL stays perfectly legible.
- **Audio notes:** A soft final resolve tone under the collapse, then silence on the hold. No
  loud outro.

## Motion notes

**Motion language used (from brief's allowed list):**
- Layout motion and queue-item insertion (Scenes 2, 5) — the signature move.
- Direct manipulation (drag reorder, Scene 2).
- Clean masking and subtle crop/reframe transitions (all scene handoffs).
- Controlled zooms — only gentle reframes toward the composer/palette (Scene 3); no constant zoom.
- Progress segments (Scene 4).
- Responsive typography confined to the lower-third band.
- Cursor / keyboard cues where useful (Scenes 1, 3).
- Restrained easing throughout (ease-out ~6–12 frames; no overshoot beyond ~2px).

**Explicitly avoided (from brief's avoid list):**
- Decorative motion with no meaning, constant zooming, excessive bounce/parallax, 3D camera
  travel, unreadable kinetic text, transitions that obscure UI, tiny text, briefly-flashed
  phrases, and platform controls covering critical content.

**Continuity rule:** Because the queue rail persists, every transition is a *reframe of one
space*, not a cut to a new place. This is the core aesthetic decision and should be preserved in
implementation.

**Pacing / readability budget:** Longest-text scene (Scene 4 caption) holds ≥ 2.5s of readable
time; reliability chips (Scene 6) hold ~1.2s each; end card holds ~3s static. All within
comfortable reading thresholds at 1080p and legible on a mobile-sized square recomposition.

**Square (1:1) recomposition:** Not a blind crop. The rail narrows to a slim indicator strip, the
conversation panel becomes the dominant square region, and the caption band moves up so text
remains centered and mobile-readable. Same beats, same timing.

---

## Audio notes

- The video is **fully understandable muted**; audio is optional and additive only.
- If used: restrained, functional UI sounds (soft discrete ticks for queue inserts and progress
  turns, gentle confirmation tones for send/connect/complete). Distinct, low-volume, non-startling.
- One soft resolving tone on the end-card collapse, then silence on the hold.
- **No** synthetic narration, **no** copyrighted music, **no** loud trailer effects/risers.
- Any bed/one-shots must be original or clearly licensed; document provenance in the video
  package's README/asset manifest before final render.
- Conservative mix: UI ticks sit well below any ambient bed; nothing peaks aggressively.
- Provide a muted verification pass (per brief's "muted verification" additional output).

## Risk assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Persistent rail + panel reduces usable width for real product screenshots (text could get small). | Medium | High (brief bans tiny text) | Use cropped, zoomed real captures inside each region; verify legibility at 1080p and in the 1:1 cut; if a screenshot can't stay legible, give it a temporary full-width reframe while keeping a slim rail indicator. |
| Continuous-reframe concept is harder to build deterministically than discrete cuts. | Medium | Medium | Implement as a fixed camera/mask timeline (keyframed), not physics; keep eases short and deterministic so renders are reproducible. |
| GitHub/YOLO boundary could blur in Scene 5 if the chip and rail visually merge. | Medium | High (core brief constraint) | Keep the "Optional GitHub app · Connected directly to ChatGPT" chip strictly on the ChatGPT panel; keep YOLO's sequence strictly on the rail; never animate credentials/tokens flowing into YOLO. |
| Scene 4 could read as an endless loop if the final segment isn't clearly resolved. | Low | High | Bar must fill to 4 of 4 and settle to a DONE/paused resting state; motion literally stops on completion. |
| Dark palette may lose contrast for accessibility. | Low | Medium | Maintain WCAG-AA contrast for all caption text against the caption band; validate with accessibility reviewer (Wave D). |
| Audio ticks feel gimmicky or push file size / distract when muted-first. | Low | Low | Keep audio optional, sparse, low; ship a clean muted verification; audio can be dropped without losing meaning. |
| Real product states for Scene 6 (draft-protect, pause-on-ambiguity) require accurate captures. | Medium | Medium | Coordinate with the deterministic-capture agent (Wave C) for real states; do not fake with abstract icons. |
| Slash actions could be mistaken for native ChatGPT commands. | Low | High | Palette is labeled "YOLO actions"; copy uses YOLO action/command framing per brief. |

**Open dependencies for production (Wave C):** real product screenshots/states for each region,
final font selection for the caption band, and confirmation of the sponsor amounts before any
"Sponsor development" CTA copy is treated as final.
